Introduction

A word of introduction. Dear reader, this is a brief summary of my life, delicately embellished with my humor. Please, read it with a wink. Just for "relaxation."

 

2003

For as long as I can remember, I've been a rather quiet child. All it took was a toy car, and I would disappear into the vast expanses of roads for hours. I could have a great time with a small toy using my imagination. However, being a quiet child also brought along shyness and limited social interactions with other peers. I won't claim I was a loner, but I wasn't the most popular kid nor the most talkative. I had a group of friends with whom I could always go out on bikes, kick a ball around, or play at the playground.

 

2007

The popular Danish building blocks quickly became an extension of my childhood imagination. Thanks to them, I could nurture my creativity. I always liked unconventional structures, and the original sets, once assembled, quickly transformed into one whole big project.

 

2010

In the fourth grade of elementary school, I managed to persuade my dad to buy me my first smartphone. Sony Xperia E. Extremely clumsy design with markedly inefficient components. But that was enough for a young mind, then a 10-year-old, to taste the infinite realm of information flow and development. So, I started playing games. Yes... Unfortunately, it didn't improve my social skills; in fact, it made them worse. While I might be able to tell my grandchildren how their granddad didn't discover the first games on Android (Fruit Ninja, Hill Climb Racing, Jetpack Joyride, Pou, Subway Surfers... etc. the list goes on), I won't hide the fact that I don't look back on that time too fondly.

 

2013

In middle school, I tried to bounce back from the "loneliness" for nearly two years. The result wasn't dazzling, but at least I wasn't the nerd sitting alone in a corner of the hallway playing on my phone. Middle school was also the time when I moved with my parents from the city to the neighboring village, to a single-family home. I could then buy my own desktop computer because I had my own room and plenty of space. Hah, admittedly, on the first day, I managed to catch my first Trojan virus in life, but those who do nothing make no mistakes, right? In my school, there were extracurricular activities where we could build robots with Lego bricks (Mindstorms). A paradise for young Kamil. Modestly speaking, one of my robots was even showcased at a fair in another middle school, and I presented its capabilities on stage. Moreover, I quickly became acquainted with the teacher leading the activities. Mr. Jacek was a tech enthusiast; we spent many hours discussing computers, IT, and various interesting technologies (that's when I learned about CERN and that I could have a skateboard like Marty McFly if only superconductors didn't consume so much liquid nitrogen.) Among the minor achievements in middle school:

  • I recreated the PacMan game in Scratch during a computer science class.
  • I proudly sat in the PE teacher's office during school assemblies and took care of the audio setup.
  • operated projectors for teachers during classes.
  • And I could boast of knowing the administrator account password - it was ******, but shh, don't tell anyone.

As you can see, the teenage years ignited a passion for computer science in me, which continues to this day.

 

2016

Only in technical high school, among my flock (computer geeks), people who understand zeros and ones (Homo cypherus), did I develop my soft skills and polish my "chatter." I consider this one of the most beautiful periods of life, especially the age of 18. Unfortunately, as we know, this beautiful carefree image was disrupted by an unexpected bat soup. Although, I won't lie. It had its charm too. One of my fondest memories is playing Minecraft with 8 people until 4 in the morning, when the sun was rising beautifully, and the roosters were crowing.

 

2021

And so, this is the summary, now I'm a student, adult life, independence, but with a bit of relaxation because I'm still in the dormitory. When I was going to college, everyone said, "it's just hard studying," "wouldn't it be better for you to get a job?" etc. Only one person said, "go, you won't find anywhere as good as in full-time studies for your parents' money," those were the words of my mom, and she was quite right. Of course, that doesn't mean I'm lounging around all day like a couch potato. It simply means that I can use this time to focus on self-improvement and finding my path in life.

Certainly, one could say that just like MS Office evolved over time, I also evolved along with it. Interesting... *blink*
